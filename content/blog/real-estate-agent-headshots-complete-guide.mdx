## What Makes a Great Real Estate Agent Headshot?

### Essential Elements

**Professional Attire**
- Business formal: Suit and tie for men, professional suit or dress for women
- Business casual: Blazer, dress shirt, professional separates
- Avoid: Casual wear, revealing clothing, outdated fashion

**Appropriate Background**
- Solid colors: White, gray, navy, or subtle gradient
- Office setting: Professional workspace or blurred office background
- Outdoor: Clean, professional setting (not casual or distracting)
- Avoid: Cluttered spaces, personal homes, casual settings

**Expression and Posing**
- Warm, approachable smile
- Direct eye contact with camera
- Head and shoulders framing
- Slight angle (not straight-on, not profile)
- Natural, confident posture

**Lighting and Quality**
- Professional studio lighting
- Even illumination on face
- No harsh shadows
- High resolution (minimum 400x400 pixels)
- Sharp focus on eyes

### Common Real Estate Headshot Mistakes

**Using Wedding or Event Photos**
While you may look great in formal wear, wedding photos don't convey real estate professionalism. Clients want to see you as a trusted advisor, not a wedding guest.

**Outdated Photos**
Using a photo from 5+ years ago is misleading. If you look significantly different in person, clients may feel deceived when you meet.

**Casual or Inappropriate Settings**
Bathroom selfies, car photos, or casual outdoor shots don't project the professionalism clients expect from someone handling their largest financial transaction.

**Group Photos or Cropped Images**
Your headshot should feature only you. Cropping group photos never looks professional and creates awkward framing.

**Overly Edited or Filtered Photos**
Heavy filters or excessive editing look unnatural and may raise questions about authenticity.
